What are the active conductances on the cell?

There are a number of active conductances placed on the different regions of the cell which underlie its electrical activity. These are listed in the table at the bottom of the widget and the colors indicate the types of ion which flows through the channels causing the conductance:

> Sodium - Na+

> Potassium - K+

> Calcium - Ca2+

> Non specific

The colored scales indicate the relative densities of the different conductances on the cell using a logarithmic scale. The red dot indicates the highest density present, and the yellow dot shows the lowest (non-zero) density.

Click on NaTs2_t (second entry from top in the table). This highlights the distribution of this transient Na conductance, with a large concentration on the soma, a lower density on the apical dendrites, and zero conductance (shows as white on the cell) on the basal dendrites and axon.

The highest densities present are for NaTa_t and K_Pst. Click on each of these in turn to show that these channels are only present on the axon (and result in this being the location of action potential initiation).

Click on Ih_apical to show how the conductance underlying the Ih current varies on the apical dendrite from low for proximal locations to high at the apical tuft.
